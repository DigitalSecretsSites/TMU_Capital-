1. Confidentiality

The microlender may not, without the express of the loan applicant / borrower and apart from disclosing relevant information to a registered credit bureau, disclose any confidential information obtained in the course of a microlending transaction other than if it required by a court order from a court with a competent jurisdiction; and the micro-lender may not, without the express written consent of the loan applicant / borrower, obtain from or to disclose to a third party, other than a registered credit bureau, the loan applicant / borrower’s credit record and payment history;

2. Legal Costs

The microlender may not collect or attempt to collect legal costs in excess of costs allowed on a party scale in terms of the Magistrates’ Courts Act. 1994 (Act No. 32 of 1944) or the High Court Act. 1990 (Act No.16 of 1990);

3. Consent to Judgement and Emolument Attachment Orders

Any consent to judgement forms or emolument attachment orders obtained prior to the borrower defaulting, is considered void and not enforceable;

4. Dispute Resolution

Complaints, which cannot be resolved between the microlender and the borrower, should be referred to NAMFISA. Attached is the complaints procedure which forms part of the agreement.

5. Cooling Off

The borrower may cancel the microlending within three (3) business days after signing of the loan agreement, provided that the loan amount and pro rata finance charges in terms of section 26 (1) of the Act at the rate applicable to that microlending transaction, be repaid simultaneously;

6. Prepayment of Instalments and Principal Debt

The borrower may make additional payment or settle the outstanding balance early in one or more payments without any penalties being levied for early settlement and that the microlender may in such event , only stipulate for demand or receive from the borrower pro rate finance charges at the rate applicable to that micro-lending transaction.

7. The Whole Contract

No addition to or variation of the agreement shall be of any force and effect unless the change reduces the borrower’s liabilities under the agreement or the change is recorded in writing and signed by both parties : and

8. Governing Law

The agreement shall be governed in all respects by the laws of the Republic of Namibia.

9. Disclosure

9.1 The micro-lender must, at every licensed premises where the microlender conducts the business –

  • 9.1.1 keep available a copy of the Microlending Act of (Act No. 7 of 2018) (“the Act”), the regulations and the standards issued under the Act which must, on request, be made available to the applicant or borrower for perusal. The microlender must further draw the attention of the loan applicant or borrower to section 23 if the Act, which provides for prohibited conduct of a microlender.
  • 9.1.2 keep available a copy of the complaint procedures as required by the standards, which must be made available to the borrower on request.
  • 9.1.3 keep copies of the complaint intake forms as required by the standards, which must be made available to the borrower on request
  • 9.1.4 display prominently, in the form of an A3 poster, the complaint Procedures as required by the standard.
  • 9.1.5 display in a form required by the standards the maximum finance charges determined by the Registerar in terms of the Usury Act; and
  • 9.1.6 display prominently the registration certificate of the microlender issued by NAMFISA.

9.2 The microlender must use standard written loan agreements, the terms of which must be approved by NAMFISA and as required by the standards, containing all the terms and conditions of the microlending transaction and clearly reflecting the rights and obligations of the borrower and the microlender.

9.3 The microlender must enter into a written loan agreement, as stipulated in subsection (2), with every borrower in the respect of each loan disbursed.

9.4 The microlender must, before the conclusion of the microlending transaction-

  • 9.4.1 perform a credit check through a registered credit bureau on a loan applicant and must carry out an affordability assessment to satisfy himself that the loan applicant is or will be able to satisfy in a timely manner allthe obligations under the loan agreement to which that loan applicant is a party, having regard to the-
    • 9.4.1.1 financial means, prospect and obligations; and
    • 9.4.1.2 probable propensity to satisfy in a timely manner all the obligations under all the loan or credit agreements to which the loan applicant is a party, as indicated by the loan applicant’s history of debt repayments, of the loan applicant;
  • 9.4.2 Provide the loan applicant with a schedule in writting setting out-
    • 9.4.2.1 the principal debt in Namibia Dollars and cents
    • 9.4.2.2 the amount of finance charges in Namibian Dollars and cents at the applicable rate over the repayment period and the elements comprising the finance charges.
    • 9.4.2.3 the total amount repayable in Namibian Dollars and cents at the current interest rate, over the payment period
    • 9.4.2.4 the finance charge rate , whether this is fixed or variable and. If variable. how it may vary;
    • 9.4.2.5 the nature and amount of any insurance, if required, including the name of the insurer and the amount of premiums payable.
    • 9.4.2.6 the penalty interest and any additional costs that would become payable in the case of a default by the loan applicant and how that would be calculated.
    • 9.4.2.7 the instalment amount in Namibian Dollars and cents. At the then current interest rate .and the number of instalments.
    • 9.4.2.8 the period of the microlending transaction; and
    • 9.4.2.9 any other costs and expenses

10. Consideration

10.1 The microlender may not charge any fee to be paid by the loan applicant in circumstances where a microlending transaction is not concluded.

10.2 The borrower may take additional payments or settle the remaining amount early in one or more payments without any penalties being levied for early settlement and the microlender may, in the event of early settlement .only stipulate for. Demand or receive from the borrower pro rata finance charges at the rate applicable to that microlending transaction.

10.3 The microlender who contravenes subsection (1) commits an offence and is liable on conviction to a fine not exceeding N$ 500 000 or to imprisonment for a period not exceeding five years. Or to both such fine and such imprisonment.
